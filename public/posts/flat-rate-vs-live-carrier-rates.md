# Flat Rate vs Live Carrier Rates: Which Is Right for Your Store?

When you set up shipping in WooCommerce, you're faced with a choice that most store owners don't spend nearly enough time thinking about: flat rate or live carrier rates?

Both work. Both have tradeoffs. And the right answer depends on what you're selling, where your customers are, and how much margin you have to absorb freight costs that don't match reality.

Here's a straight breakdown.

---

## What flat rate shipping is

Flat rate shipping means you charge every customer the same amount for freight — regardless of what they ordered, how much it weighs, or where they live.

You might charge $12 for standard delivery on everything. Or you might use a simple table: $10 for orders under 5kg, $18 for orders over 5kg. Either way, the rate is predetermined and doesn't change based on the actual cost of shipping that specific order.

**When flat rate works:**

- Your products are similar in size and weight (e.g. you sell one type of thing)
- Your customers are mostly in the same region (e.g. a local Melbourne business)
- Your margins are high enough to absorb the difference between your flat rate and actual freight costs
- You're early stage and just need something in place to get selling

**When flat rate breaks down:**

- You sell a wide range of products with different weights and sizes
- You ship nationally and freight costs vary significantly by zone
- You're losing money on bulky or regional orders
- You're overcharging metro customers and they're noticing

---

## What live carrier rates are

Live carrier rates calculate the exact freight cost for each order at the moment of checkout — based on the customer's postcode, the weight and dimensions of what they ordered, and the rates from your carrier's rate card.

Instead of "$12 flat", the customer sees "Allied Express — Standard $14.80" or "StarTrack — Express $28.50". The number is specific to their order and their location.

**When live rates work:**

- You ship products of varying size and weight
- You ship nationally and want to charge what it actually costs
- You want to show multiple carrier options at checkout
- You have negotiated rates with a carrier and want to use them

**When live rates require more setup:**

- You need your carrier's rate card and zone file loaded into a system that can calculate from them
- You need a plugin or tool that can do the calculation at checkout
- If your carrier rates change, you need to update the rate card

---

## The margin maths

Here's a concrete example. Say you ship nationally and your average freight cost is $18, but it ranges from $11 (Melbourne metro) to $48 (remote WA).

With a $18 flat rate:
- Melbourne customer pays $18, actual cost $11 → you absorb $7 overcharge or pocket it
- Remote WA customer pays $18, actual cost $48 → you lose $30 on every order

With live rates:
- Melbourne customer pays $11.50 → fair, competitive, no surprise
- Remote WA customer pays $48.00 → covers your cost, no subsidy

The flat rate approach means you're either overcharging customers who notice (and leave) or subsidising customers you can't afford to. Live rates fix both.

---

## The middle ground: zone-based flat rates

Some stores use a compromise: different flat rates per zone (e.g. $10 metro, $18 regional, $28 remote). This is better than a single flat rate but still requires manual maintenance and doesn't account for order weight.

It's a reasonable stepping stone but not a long-term solution for stores with diverse product ranges.

---

## Which should you choose?

**Choose flat rate if** you're just starting out, your products are uniform in size and weight, and you mostly ship to one region. It's simple and it works well enough in those conditions.

**Switch to live rates if** you're shipping nationally, your product range varies in weight or size, you're losing money on freight, or customers are abandoning checkout because your flat rate feels arbitrary or high.
