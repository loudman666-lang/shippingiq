# Allied Express, StarTrack, Hunter Express: Which Carrier Is Right for Your Store?

Choosing a freight carrier is one of the more consequential decisions you'll make for your ecommerce business. Get it right and you have reliable delivery, competitive rates, and happy customers. Get it wrong and you're dealing with claims, complaints, and pricing that eats your margin.

Here's a practical look at three major Australian domestic freight carriers — Allied Express, StarTrack, and Hunter Express — and how to think about which one suits your store.

---

## A note before we start

No carrier is universally best. The right choice depends on what you're shipping, where your customers are, what you've negotiated, and what your depot coverage looks like. This guide gives you a framework for thinking about the decision — not a definitive ranking.

Also worth noting: most established ecommerce stores use more than one carrier. Different carriers for different product types, zones, or order values is a perfectly legitimate (and often optimal) strategy.

---

## Allied Express

Allied Express is one of Australia's largest independent freight networks, with a strong focus on same-day and next-day metro delivery as well as interstate road freight.

**Strengths:**
- Strong metro coverage in all major capitals
- Competitive on bulky and heavy freight
- Flexible consignment options including tail-lift delivery
- Good for furniture, homewares, and large goods
- Depot network across all states

**Considerations:**
- Regional coverage can be slower than metro
- Rate cards tend to use a base charge + per-kg model, which requires a bit more calculation
- Surcharge schedule can be complex — fuel levy, residential, tailgate, overlength all apply

**Best for:** Stores shipping bulky or heavy goods nationally, particularly furniture, homewares, sporting equipment, or anything that benefits from tail-lift capability.

---

## StarTrack

StarTrack is owned by Australia Post and operates one of the most extensive freight networks in the country, with particularly strong coverage in regional and remote areas.

**Strengths:**
- Exceptional regional and remote coverage — hard to beat for WA, NT, and rural Queensland
- Strong integration with Australia Post's last-mile network
- Reliable track and trace
- Good for parcels and smaller consignments as well as pallets
- Well-known brand — customers trust the name

**Considerations:**
- Can be more expensive for metro-to-metro freight compared to independents
- Better suited to parcel freight than heavy bulk freight
- Transit times to remote areas are still subject to geographic reality

**Best for:** Stores with a national customer base, particularly those shipping to regional and remote Australia regularly. Also good if you're currently using Australia Post and want to step up to a carrier with freight capability.

---

## Hunter Express

Hunter Express operates primarily on the eastern seaboard, with a network focused on NSW, QLD, VIC, SA, and ACT. They're known for competitive pricing and reliable service within their core coverage area.

**Strengths:**
- Competitive rates on eastern seaboard lanes
- Good performance for NSW and QLD freight in particular
- Strong for palletised freight and larger consignments
- Responsive account management for businesses with regular volume

**Considerations:**
- Coverage in WA, NT, and Tasmania is more limited — often relies on linehaul partners for these lanes
- Less recognised brand than StarTrack or Allied in some markets
- Better suited to B2B freight than high-volume B2C parcel delivery

**Best for:** Stores based in NSW or QLD shipping primarily to eastern seaboard customers. Also good for businesses with a mix of B2B and B2C freight who need competitive pallet rates.

---

## How to choose

Here are the questions that matter most:

**Where are your customers?** If the majority of your orders go to metro addresses in Melbourne, Sydney, and Brisbane, most carriers will serve you well and the decision comes down to rates and reliability. If you ship regularly to regional WA, Tasmania, or the NT, StarTrack's coverage advantage matters a lot.

**What are you shipping?** Heavy, bulky goods (furniture, gym equipment, building materials) suit carriers with tail-lift capability and competitive per-kg rates on high weights — Allied Express is strong here. Smaller parcels and mixed freight suit a broader network carrier.

**What have you negotiated?** Your contracted rates matter more than the carrier's brand. A 15% better rate with one carrier often outweighs a marginal service advantage with another. Get quotes from at least two carriers before committing.

**Do you need multiple carriers?** Many WooCommerce stores benefit from routing different order types to different carriers. Bulky items to Allied, parcels to StarTrack, eastern seaboard pallets to Hunter. This is more complex to manage but gives you the best rates and coverage across your full range.
